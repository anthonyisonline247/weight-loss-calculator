❓ What is TDEE?

Your TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is the total number of calories your body burns each day — including exercise. Eat below this number to lose weight. The closer your deficit is to 500 cal/day, the more sustainable and muscle-preserving your results will be. Extreme deficits (>1000 cal/day) can cause muscle loss, fatigue, and metabolic slowdown.

🛠️ Science-Backed Plateau Breakers

1. Diet Break (1–2 weeks at maintenance) Eating at TDEE for 1–2 weeks resets leptin levels and reduces cortisol, often kicking off faster loss afterwards. (Byrne et al., 2017 — the "MATADOR" study)
2. Reverse Dieting Gradually increase calories by 50–100/week over several weeks to restore metabolic rate before cutting again.
3. Protein Reset Bump protein to 0.9–1g per lb of body weight. Higher protein has a thermic effect (burns 25–30% of its own calories during digestion).
4. Calorie Cycling Eat maintenance on hard training days, deficit on rest days. Prevents adaptive thermogenesis better than a fixed deficit.
5. Strength Training Adding or increasing resistance training preserves muscle during deficits, maintaining a higher RMR long-term.
⚠️ What NOT to do: Don't cut calories further below 1200 (women) or 1500 (men). This worsens metabolic slowdown and risks nutrient deficiencies.

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the calories your body burns at complete rest — just to keep your organs running. TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is BMR multiplied by your activity level. It represents total daily calorie burn including exercise and daily movement. You should base your weight loss calorie target on TDEE, not BMR.
